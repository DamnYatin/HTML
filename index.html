Khajuraho Temples

Khajuraho is a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its stunning medieval Hindu and Jain temples. Built between 950 and 1050 CE by the Chandela dynasty, these temples are renowned for their intricate sculptures and architectural brilliance. The temples showcase nagara-style architecture and depict various aspects of life, mythology, and spirituality.

Sanchi Stupa

The Great Sanchi Stupa is one of the oldest stone structures in India and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE, it houses Buddhist relics and is surrounded by intricately carved gateways (toranas). This sacred site represents the pinnacle of Buddhist architecture and art, attracting pilgrims and tourists from around the world.

Pachmarhi Hill Station

Pachmarhi is the only hill station in Madhya Pradesh, nestled in the Satpura Range at an elevation of 1,067 meters. Known as "Satpura Ki Rani" (Queen of Satpura), it offers breathtaking waterfalls, ancient caves, and lush green valleys. Popular attractions include Bee Fall, Jata Shankar Caves, and Dhupgarh - the highest point in the state.

Bandhavgarh National Park

Bandhavgarh National Park is one of India's premier tiger reserves, known for having the highest density of Bengal tigers in the world. Spread across 450 square kilometers, the park features diverse flora and fauna, ancient caves with Brahmi inscriptions, and the historic Bandhavgarh Fort. It's a paradise for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers.
